Over eight years as the sole designer at Copeland, I led UX across five products — each with its own constraint profile, audience, and failure modes. Some used fixed-segment displays, some touchscreens, some just three buttons and almost no room for instruction. Hospitality thermostats had to be understood from across a hotel room by guests who may not speak the language on the label.

Every surface has to work on its own terms while still feeling like it belongs to the same family. That is not a visual style problem — it is a decision-making problem. Every call either reinforces the system or quietly undermines it.
