Before touching the system itself, I went through what was already there: what each product did well, where it frustrated people, and which workflows users had wrapped their SOPs around. That last part mattered most. In a regulated environment, you cannot move an interface around until you understand what depends on it. So Kelvin had to work with what already existed rather than sweep it away, and that constraint is the thing the whole system is built on.
