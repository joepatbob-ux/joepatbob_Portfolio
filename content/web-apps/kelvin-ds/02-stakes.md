The stakes vary across the portfolio, but they are never low.

---

TempTrak users are medical and pharmaceutical facility managers working under regulatory oversight. Their temperature logs are compliance documents, and acknowledging an alarm is a documented procedure that may be tied to SOPs referencing specific screens. Change the interface and you are not just asking people to retrain; you can invalidate a process that exists for regulatory reasons.

The hardware raises the stakes further. Temperature, humidity, and door sensors feed these systems around the clock, so a UI change that trips up a monitoring workflow does more than confuse someone. It can open a gap in the chain of custody for materials that are not allowed to be compromised.

---

That is why the "if it ain't broke" instinct here was rational rather than merely stubborn. Getting it wrong did not cost a bad review; it cost something real. Any modernization plan had to take that seriously instead of designing around it.
