  "heading": "Removing a button that was doing someone else's job."
}
---

The installation flow is one of the highest-stakes screens in the app. If a homeowner misreads their wiring in the wire picker, the app infers the wrong equipment configuration, and that is not a cosmetic mistake; it can leave someone with a system that will not run.

The legacy flow put two buttons at the bottom of every screen, Previous and Next. But iOS and Android both hand you reliable back navigation at the top of the screen, which is where people already reach for it. A second back button down at the bottom did not make anything safer. It added weight, pulled attention in two directions, and stranded the primary action between two competing controls. Dropping Previous let Continue stand on its own. The help link moved up above it as a real tappable target, and the progress indicator moved into the navigation bar, so you can see where you are in the install without spending screen space on it.

App Store reviewers picked up on it without being asked. One long-time user called the step-by-step guidance "a little touch that made me fall in love with it." You get responses like that when the interface leans on what people already know instead of fighting it.
